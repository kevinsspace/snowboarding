Dragon Ball is a world-renowned Japanese media franchise that started as a manga series and quickly expanded into one of the most popular anime of all time. Here's what you need to know: Core Story Dragon Ball follows the life of Son Goku, a young boy with a monkey tail who possesses extraordinary strength. His adventures begin when he meets Bulma, a girl on a quest to find the seven mystical Dragon Balls. These orbs, when gathered, summon a wish-granting dragon. Goku and Bulma's journey leads to many more friends, epic battles, martial arts tournaments, and a whole lot of power-ups! Why It's So Popular Iconic Characters: Dragon Ball features a massive cast of beloved characters, each with unique designs, personalities, and fighting styles. From Goku and Vegeta to Piccolo, Krillin, and many more, these characters become like family to viewers. Action-Packed: Dragon Ball is famous for its over-the-top fight scenes filled with energy blasts, martial arts mastery, and transformations that lead to ever-increasing power levels. Humor and Heart: While action is a major draw, Dragon Ball also has a surprisingly comedic side alongside touching moments of friendship and self-sacrifice. Generational Appeal: Dragon Ball has been running in some form since 1984. It holds a special nostalgic place for older fans while continuing to enthrall new generations with its action and timeless themes. The Broader Dragon Ball Universe Dragon Ball: The original series, covering Goku's childhood and early adventures. Dragon Ball Z: Focuses on Goku's adult life, introducing intergalactic threats and the iconic Super Saiyan transformations. Dragon Ball GT: A non-canon sequel series (not written by the original creator). Dragon Ball Super: The official continuation of the story, picking up after the events of Dragon Ball Z. Movies: Numerous films expanding the world and introducing unique villains and stories. Where to Start If you're new to Dragon Ball, the most common starting points are: Dragon Ball: For the pure origin story with a stronger comedic focus. Dragon Ball Z: If you want the intense action and iconic moments most people associate with the franchise. Dragon Ball Z Kai: A remastered version of Dragon Ball Z with less filler and better pacing.
